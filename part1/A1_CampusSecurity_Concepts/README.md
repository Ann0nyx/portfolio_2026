# A1 – Security Concepts used on campus 

## Security Concepts Identified –

### 1)Confidentiality – Student/Staff ID access systems

In University of Western Australia’s Campus, it is observed that confidentiality is maintained by using access control systems restricting access to authorised individuals. Libraries, buildings and labs have these access system that provide access only when scanned by a valid student ID. The Reid library also restricts access to the upper floors to outsiders after staffed hours, where only someone with a campus card can access these floors. This prevents unauthorised access to sensitive area thereby protecting physical assets and personal data.


### 2)Integrity – Serveillance cameras in libraries

In campus libraries such as Reid, It is observed to have surveillance cameras around multiple areas on different floors. These cameras ensure that activities within the library is strictly monitored, discouraging tampering or damaging of resources such as books, computers and study equipment. Individuals are also less likely to damage or alter shared resources when under surveillance, preserving their original condition maintaining integrity.


### 3)Availability – Emergency exits and safety infrastructure

Availability is ensured by the presence of emergency exits and safety systems across the University Campus building such as the Computer Science block, Reid library, Barry J Library and all other buildings. These emergency exits are kept accessible at all times allowing individuals to evacuate quickly and safely incase of danger or emergencies such as fire or security threats. These exits are clearly marked and unobstructed to ensure immediate use without restriction. Additionally, emergency alarm systems support visibility and support during critical situations. These make sure safety mechanisms are always operational and accessible when needed, which is a key aspect of availability in physical security concept.
