# A2 – Security Concepts in Public spaces

## Security concepts Identified -

### 1)Confidentiality - ATMs authentication

In ATMs, confidentiality is maintained through authentication mechanisms. These machines require users to insert their bank card and enter a personal Identification number(PIN) that is only know to the user before accessing their account. This ensures only authorised individuals can access their sensitive financial information. Furthermore, ATM pin pads are designed with additional shielding to prevent others from observing the PIN during entry. This concept of authentication and physical protection prevents unauthorised access to personal and financial data, thereby maintaining confidentiality in a public environment.

### 2)Integrity - Transperth officers validating tickets and smart rider cards

In public transport areas, Integrity is observed to maintained by Transperth officers who checks tickets and smart rider cards of individual. They are also seen monitoring if each person tags on and off correctly when entering or exiting the train stations. By verifying fares and enforcing these rules, they  prevent misuse such as fare evasion or use of invalid cards. This also ensures that travel records and payment systems remain accurate and trustworthy. Additionally , ticket validation and scanners help ensure that fare data is recorded accurately without unauthorised modifications, maintain the integrity of the system.

### 3)Availability – intercoms on trains and platforms

Availability is ensured through emergency intercom systems located inside trains and station platforms. These intercoms allow passengers to swiftly contact the driver or control centre incase of emergencies such as medical issues, safety concerns or suspicious activity. For instance, pressing this emergency button establishes immediate communication with authorised personal ensuring assistance is available without delay. These systems operate continuously, making sure help is accessible to all at all times which is essential for maintaining safety in public transport surroundings.
