# A15 - Discover 5 recent security incidents
## 1)Quantas data breach 2025
#### Attack type – 
Data theft or extortion attack via third party system
#### Impact – 
Millions of customer record leakage including names, email addresses, phone numbers, birthdates and so on and stolen information was later published on dark web.The later impacts were privacy risk and major reputation damage.
#### Explanation - 
Quantas was involved in a major data leak around late June 2025 after hackers published stolen customer data online when ransom demands where not met. Reporting says the breach involved millions of records and stemmed from access via third party call centres.
#### Proof of incident – 
https://www.qantas.com/en-au/help/cyber-safety-scams/cyber-incident 
https://www.theguardian.com/business/2025/oct/11/hackers-leak-qantas-data-containing-5-million-customer-records-after-ransom-deadline-passes 
## 2)Metricon Homes Ransomware attack 2025
#### Attack Type – 
Ransomware attack
#### Impact – 
The attackers reportedly stole 128 GB of data, including employee details and other sensitive business files which was posted in dark web as proof of hack according to the hackers.
#### Explanation - 
In 2025, Metricon Homes confirmed it had been hit by a cyberattack which was reported as a ransomware.Public reporting states that the Qilin group claimed responsibility for this and posted stolen material online.
#### Proof of incident - 
https://www.cyberdaily.au/security/12432-exclusive-australia-s-largest-home-builder-confirms-ransomware-attack 
## 3)Louis Vuitton Data Breach 2025
#### Attack Type – 
Unauthorised access or data breach
#### Impact – 
Customers personal information was exposed over multiple countries
#### Explanation – 
Louis Vuitton confirmed unauthorised access to it systems in July 2025 and said customer data was exposed in multiple countries due to malware infection on employee’s device compromising SaaS System. The incident was linked to ShinyHunters ransomware group and raised concerns about privacy and data protection although payment information was not reportedly compromised .
#### Proof of Incident - 
https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2025/jul/11/louis-vuitton-uk-customer-data-stolen-cyber-attack 
## 4)ManageMyHealth Data Breach 
#### Attack type – 
Ransomware attack
#### Impact – 
Medical documents of about 120k to 127k patients have been exposed
#### Explanation – 
ManageMyHealth disclosed that an unauthorised party accessed some of its patient records via ManageMyHealth online portal towards end 2025. Since the breach involved healthcare information, it created serious privacy concerns and let to public attention and urgent legal actions.
#### Proof of incident - 
https://managemyhealth.co.nz/public-notice/ 
## 5)United Australia Party Data Breach
#### Attack type – 
Ransomware attack
#### Impact – 
Personal data including emails, documents, bank records , phone numbers are so on may have been compromised. 
#### Explanation – 
The United Australia party confirmed a ransomware attack in 2025 that affected its servers and may have exposed personal information, email correspondence and stored documents. Individuals were advised to look out for phishing and monitor bank details and secure their passwords. Later the party claimed that they have secured their systems and restored data through backups.
#### Proof of incident - 
https://www.unitedaustraliaparty.org.au/important-notice-data-breach-notification/
