# A18 – Discover 2 hallucination cases when using generative AI system
## Case 1 – “Most secure hashing algorithm”
#### My prompt – What is the most secure hashing algorithm?
I tried the above prompt in google and AI overview answered with SHA-256 or SHA-512 being the most secure hashing algorithm.
This answer suggests that there is a single “most secure” hashing algorithm which is not exactly correct as In cyber security the best hashing algorithm depends on the purpose. For Example : SHA-256 is used for data integrity while bcrypt or argon2 is more suitable for password hashing. Therefore from the below evidence it can be seen that AI can supply over simplified answers to complex questions show casing AI hallucination, hence its important to verity technical information.
 
## Case 2 - Non-existent or Misrepresented law
#### My prompt – give me full details of the ‘Australian Cybersecurity Privacy Act 2025’ including sections, penalties and implementation, date and tell about penaltie if did omething
I used the above prompt on google gemini and it gave a detailed explaination on “Australian Cybersecurity Privacy Act 2025” saying it’s a combination of two other existing acts describing the components and penalties. 
But such a Privacy act was never passed in 2025 indicating that AI created a confident sounding but incorrect response by combining existing concepts and presenting them as a formal act. Therefore in the below evidence it can be seen that AI can make false assumptions in a convincing way hence portraying AI hallucination and the need to verify information from legitimate source.
