# A16 - Discover 3 local security incidents
## 1)University of Western Australia data breach
Attack type – Data breach (unauthorised access to password information)

Impact – All staffs and students were required to reset their password leading to widespread access issues and temporary lockouts from UWA systems.

Explanation – In August 2025, University of Western Australia detected unauthorised access to university password information. As a security measure, the university locked out staff and student from its system and required everyone to reset their passwords and set up Multifactor Authentication as needed before regaining access. This breach although it did not reportedly get access to anything else other than passwords, compromised system availability for a period of time as student were unable to access their Student Connect accounts.

Proof of Incident - https://www.abc.net.au/news/2025-08-11/university-of-western-australia-uwa-suffers-major-data-breach/105636074 

## 2)Legal Practice board WA cyber incident

Attack Type – Ransomware attack

Impact – Some systems where taken offline, including online web servers and later investigation showed that sensitive information may have been breached.

Explanation – In May 2025, Legal Practice board of WA experienced a cyber incident that disrupted its systems and forced it to use manual workarounds. Later investigations reported the attack as ransomware linked to Dire Wolf group, which claimed to have stolen data. The board later said it was notifying affected individuals and how it had to worked to secure its systems and reduce any further risks.

Proof of Incident - https://www.lpbwa.org.au/boardsheet-june-2025 

## 3)University of Norte Dame cyber incident
Attack Type – Ransomware Attack

Impact – The incident affected university systems and caused problems with enrolment and accessing class timetables. The attackers also claimed to have stolen mass amounts of data.

Explanation – In early 2025, The University of Norte Dame confirmed it was investigating a cyber incident at its University campus. Later reporting showed that Fog Ransomware group claimed responsibility and alleged that over 62GB of data had been stolen including tax file numbers, student and employee informations. The systems were disrupted for long periods of time even after semester started.

Proof of incident - https://www.notredame.edu.au/news-items/statement-from-the-university-of-notre-dame-australia
